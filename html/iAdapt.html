Overview

During my last year of university, I joined a neurology research group on campus to conduct independent research. I quickly became involved in an onoing project whose goal was to investigate visual adaptation and to understand the feasibility and reliability of using online tools to reach a large subject pool.

My Role

1. Edited and managed our experiment website through various iterations of the project.
2. Managed subjects: responded to questions, handled subject payment, and resolved website issues based on user feedback.
4. Created graphs and visualizations.
5. Started and monitored all experiments!

Tools Used

1. Amazon Mechanical Turk: used for recruiting, filtering, and paying subjects.
2. Experiment Site: where subjects are redirected to participate in the actual experiment.
3. Matlab: for analyzing subject data and calculating subject payment.

Challenges

1. Learning how to edit the online experiment in a very public way. Once a new experimental trial was live, I was immediately made aware of any mistakes and needed to correct them as soon as possible.
2. Keeping the relatively long and tedious experiment within a resonable amount of time. Going through the experiment myself was a very useful way to get a sense of this.
