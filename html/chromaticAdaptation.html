INTRODUCTION

Chromatic adaptation is a well documented phenomenon in color perception in which staring at a certain color for a period of time affects the way that and other colors are seen subsequently. Specifically, detection of that same color seems diminished while detection of the opposing color is increased. While the mechanism underlying this effect is not fully understood, this little MATLAB tutorial will try to explain and model it simply.

OPPONENT COLOR THEORY

Color opponency is a theory that describes a mechanism having to do with color that operates above the level of cones. To review, there are three cone types in the retina of your eye that respond to color: S (short), M (medium) and L (long). Each of these has a frequency range in which the the cone responds which together make up the visible spectrum. According to color opponent theory, some cells respond most to red, green, blue, or yellow.
